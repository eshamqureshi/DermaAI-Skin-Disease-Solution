
# DermaAI: Skin Disease Solution

An AI-powered computer vision platform designed for real-time dermatological analysis and specialist orchestration. The system processes clinical imagery to generate instant metric tallies, trigger critical triage alerts, and power an interactive management dashboard where medical specialists can seamlessly launch virtual diagnostic calls.

### Core Stack
* **AI/Inference:** High-throughput computer vision 2 stage pipeline optimized for medical image classification and localized lesion detection.
* **Architecture:** Event-driven, containerized microservices engineered for secure, low-latency telemetry processing and real-time signaling.

###Abstract
Skin diseases represent a significant global health challenge, affecting approximately 75% of individuals worldwide, with prevalence rates as high as 67% in Asia and 28% in the United States. Despite the widespread incidence, nearly 73% of those affected do not seek medical attention due to barriers such as busy schedules, long travel distances, fear of disease exposure, and physical disabilities. Early and accurate diagnosis is crucial for effective treatment, yet current detection methods are often hindered by the visual similarity of various skin conditions, leading to frequent misdiagnoses. To address these issues, this research introduces DermaAI, a web-based diagnostic and teleconsultation platform integrating advanced deep learning for automated skin disease classification and virtual care delivery. Two custom datasets were developed: a binary classification dataset (7,421 images) for skin/non-skin distinction and a multi-class disease dataset (7,864 images) covering Acne, Psoriasis, Fungal Infection, Vitiligo, and Healthy classes. A custom Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) employing the Hard-Swish activation and sparse categorical cross-entropy loss achieved 98.63% accuracy in binary classification and 81.34% accuracy with an 81.28% F1-score in multi-class disease prediction, outperforming benchmark models such as VGG16, DenseNet, and InceptionV3. The system architecture features dedicated dashboards for patients, doctors, and administrators, supporting role-based functionalities, including AI-driven diagnosis, secure video consultations, appointment scheduling, and content management. By combining high-performing CNN models with an intuitive and secure web interface, DermaAI demonstrates the potential of AI-assisted teledermatology to enhance accessibility, improve diagnostic accuracy, and promote equitable healthcare delivery. Successful implementation validates the feasibility of integrating deep learning and telemedicine as a scalable framework for real-world dermatological applications.
